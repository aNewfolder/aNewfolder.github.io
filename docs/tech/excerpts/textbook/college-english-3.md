# 大学英语（三）

1. What makes it rather disturbing was the **arbitrary** circumstances both of my arrest and my subsequent fate in court.

   Arbitrary: based on one's own opinion only, not on reason

2. But as soon as my father found out what had happened, he hired a very good **solicitor**.（初级）律师

3. Given the **obscure** nature of the charge, I feel sure that if I had come from a different background, and had really been unemployed, there is every chance that I would have been found guilty.

   Obscure: not clearly seen or understood

4. **Presumably** that I should have looked outraged and said something like, "Look here, do you know who you're talking to? I am a highly successful student with a brilliant academic record."

   Presumably: probably

5. The oldest didn't take more than a second to deliver his smug answer.

6. Not to be **outdone** by his younger siblings, the 9-year-old said, "It could also be the orange because the other two grow on vines."

   Outdo: do more or better than sb.

7. He found that they'd all **contracted** a similar but milder disease, cow pox, which vaccinated them against the deadly smallpox.

   Contract: catch or develop (an illness)

8. They were refocusing the **parameters**.

   Parameter: limiting factor or characteristic; limit

9. This is not meant to be a **critique** of schools. 评论、评论文章

10. Businesses must be able to download volumes of data **instantaneously**. 即时的，瞬间的

11. Nor do I teach because I think I know answers, or because I have knowledge I feel **compelled** to share.

    Compel: force

12. I teach because I enjoy finding ways of getting myself and my students out of the **ivory** tower and into the real world. 象牙

13. But we also set up a corporation, borrowed money, purchased a **run-down** house and practiced self-reliance by renovating it. old and broken or in bad condition

14. There is Jacqui, a cleaning woman who knows more by **intuition** than most of us learn by analysis. 直觉

15. It **took me to task** for my comments on the Los Angeles Dodgers and argued that I had got everything wrong.

    Take sb. to task: criticize severely (for sth.)

16. Because I have a physical handicap, it took me five years to complete my **associate's** degree. 大专文凭

17. This was obviously an **elaborate** hoax. carefully planned and organized

18. Who, in her supposed condition, could cover a baseball team without the best equipment and help?

19. I was shocked into stillness by what I saw.

20. Some years before, when we sold our **ranch** and moved to town, Mother had decided to open a day nursery. a very large farm for raising horses, cattle or sheep

21. "I know you do," Dad said **evenly**. calmly; peacefully

22. My shock and embarrassment at finding Mother in tears on Wednesday was a perfect **index** of how little I understood the pressures on her. sign or indication

23. He lay still in the bed and seemed very **detached from** what was going on. show no interest in, be indifferent to

24. We **flushed** a **covey** of **quail** under a high clay bank with **overhanging** brush and I killed two as they went out of sight over the top of the bank.

    Flush（使）（鸟）惊飞；Covey a small flock or group (of small birds)；Quail 鹌鹑；Overhang hang over or stand out over

25. Coming out while you were poised unsteadily on the icy, **springy** brush they made difficult shooting and I killed two, missed five, and started back pleased to have found a covey close to the house and happy there were so many left to find on another day. flexible 有弹性的

26. The important thing is, Gig, that now I can teach you because you have the tools. And, **in all modesty**, I know a lot about the trade. 谦虚的说，毫不夸张的说

27. The **siren** also reaches its top and then suddenly goes off and there is absolute dead silence for a long moment. penetrating whistle as a warning

28. Well, you can understand why we **blew our tops** a little—explode with anger

29. She wasn't used to having Paula talk to her in such an **intimate** tone. 亲密的，个人的

30. He stood there with the keen, unrevealing smile on his face.

31. **Not for love or money.** Not by any means.

32. You'll never **amount to** anything if you spend your time that way!

    Never amount to anything: never become successful in life；Amount to: develop into; be equal to 达到，等于，相当于

33. Or it was considered an unhealthy escape from real life and its duties.

34. He **maintained** that "you can imagine your future." 断言

35. Picture yourself as winning, and that will contribute immeasurably to success.

36. The resulting memory traces will **supposedly** start affecting your everyday life. They will help lead you to the attainment of your goals. presumably; probably

37. Song of **Defiance**

    Defiance: open disobedience or resistance; refusal to give way to authority or opposition

38. When you walk the cobbled mist-**shrouded** streets of Terezin in the Czech Republic, your mind fills with images of the village sixty years ago, when it was a Nazi concentration camp packed with desperate and dying Jews.

    Shroud: cover and hide

39. Terezin had been a **perverse** kind of showcase. In contrast to Auschwitz, Treblinka and other **extermination** camps, the Nazis designed the town near Prague to fool the world. For much of World War II, Nazi **propaganda** suggested that Jews there enjoyed a life of leisure, even using captive Jewish filmmakers to craft a movie showing "happy" Jews listening to lectures and basking in the sun.

    Perverse behaving in an unreasonable way；Extermination 消灭，灭绝，根除；Propaganda ideas, false or true information, etc. spread about officially esp. by a government

40. Yet the camp made **concessions** for propaganda purposes. 妥协、让步

41. Charismatic, with a striking face and wavy, dark hair, Schaechter was just beginning to **make a name for himself** in the rich cultural mix of prewar Prague. establish one's reputation

42. Everything that Schaechter wanted to say lay camouflaged within the Latin words of the Requiem, with its themes of God's **wrath** and human liberation. Schaechter had access to no musical instruments except a broken harmonium found in a rubbish **heap**.

    Wrath extreme anger；Heap number of things or mass of material lying piled up

43. Since there was only one single **score**, the singers had to memorize their parts, in Latin, a language that few besides Schaechter understood. 乐谱

44. The first performance took place for prisoners gathered in a former **gymnasium**. gym

45. They **gnawed** at it from **sheer** hunger.

    Gnaw bite sth. hard continually until it is worn away；Sheer complete; thorough; utter

46. The day of wrath, the day shall dissolve the world in ash. … What trembling there shall be when the judge shall come. … Nothing shall remain **unavenged**.

    Unavenged 未得报仇的，未得雪耻的；Avenge take or get revenge for

47. After he **retired**, a curious thing happened. 退下、离开

48. But in the meantime, for a brief spell, now that…

49. To most people the term technology conjures up images of smoky steel **mills** or noisy machines. factory or workshop

50. But we have, with the passage of time, invented all sorts of social devices to hasten the process.
